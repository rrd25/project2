The History of the Microprocessor

The First Microprocessor

A microprocessor incorporates the functions of a Central Processing Unit (CPU) on a integrated circuit (IC).

In 1970, Intel introduced the first dynamic RAM, which increased IC memory. These products made Intel an innovative company. The first commerically available microprocessor was the Intel 4004. The project was started when a Japanese calculator manufacturer, Busicom, asked intel to build a chipset for high performance desktop calculators. The first known advertisement for the Intel 4004 was broadcast on November 15, 1971.
